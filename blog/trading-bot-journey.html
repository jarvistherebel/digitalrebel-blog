I Built a Trading Bot: Here's What Happened

A few months ago, I decided to build an automated trading bot. Not because I thought I'd get rich overnight, but because I wanted to understand the intersection of AI, automation, and financial markets.

Here's the honest, unfiltered story of what happened.

Why Build a Trading Bot?

I'm an automation junkie. If something can be automated, I want to automate it. Trading seemed like the perfect candidate - it's data-driven, rule-based (in theory), and runs 24/7. Plus, I was already interested in crypto markets.

The Tech Stack

I built the bot in Python because it has the best ecosystem for this kind of thing:

  • Python - Core language
  • ccxt - Exchange connectivity
  • pandas - Data analysis
  • Custom sentiment analysis - Using AI to gauge market mood
  • Technical indicators - RSI, MACD, moving averages

The Strategy

I started with a simple approach: combine technical indicators with sentiment analysis. The idea was that technical analysis alone misses the human element, while sentiment alone misses the chart patterns. Together, they'd give a more complete picture.

What Went Right

The bot actually worked. It executed trades automatically, managed risk with stop-losses, and ran 24/7 without intervention. The sentiment analysis added a genuinely useful signal - it caught several moves that pure technical analysis would have missed.

What Went Wrong

Plenty. The first version had a bug that placed orders at the wrong size. Fortunately, I caught it during paper trading. Slippage was worse than expected on smaller exchanges. And the bot occasionally got confused by sudden market moves that didn't match any historical pattern.

The market humbles everyone eventually. The goal isn't to be right all the time - it's to make more when you're right than you lose when you're wrong.

Lessons Learned

  1. Paper trade first. Always. I found three critical bugs during paper trading that would have cost real money.
  2. Risk management is everything. The strategy matters less than how much you risk per trade.
  3. Markets are humbling. No matter how good your system is, there will be losing streaks.
  4. Automation doesn't mean hands-off. You still need to monitor, adjust, and improve.
  5. Start small. Even when you're confident, start with minimal position sizes.

What's Next

The bot is still running and I'm actively improving it. I'm working on better sentiment analysis, more sophisticated position sizing, and multi-exchange support. I'll keep sharing updates on the blog.
