Building a 10% Daily Trading Bot

The complete technical breakdown of my aggressive trading system

⚠️ Risk Warning

This is a high-risk trading strategy. Past performance doesn't guarantee future results. I'm sharing this for educational purposes. Never trade with money you can't afford to lose.

The Goal

Build an automated trading system that targets 10%+ daily returns across crypto and forex markets. Aggressive? Yes. Possible? Let's find out.

The Strategy Stack

1. Multi-Strategy Approach

The bot doesn't rely on a single strategy. It combines:

Momentum Chasing (40% of signals)

  • Identify strong trends using EMA crossovers
  • Enter on pullbacks to moving averages
  • Ride trends with trailing stops
  • Best in: Strong trending markets

Mean Reversion (35% of signals)

  • RSI oversold (< 30) for buys, overbought (> 70) for sells
  • Bollinger Band bounces
  • Quick 2-5% scalps
  • Best in: Ranging, choppy markets

Sentiment Boost (25% of signals)

  • CryptoPanic API for news sentiment
  • Social volume spikes
  • Fear/greed indicators
  • Best in: News-driven moves

Risk Management

This is where most bots fail. Here's my approach:

Position Sizing

  • Max 5% per trade - Never bet the farm
  • Max 20% total exposure - Always keep cash
  • Scaling in: 2% initial, add 1% on confirmation

Stop Losses

  • Hard stop: 2% loss per trade (automatic)
  • Trailing stop: 1.5% below highest price
  • Time stop: Close if no move in 4 hours

Portfolio Protection

  • Daily loss limit: 5% max drawdown = stop trading
  • Correlation check: Don't hold similar assets
  • Volatility filter: Skip if ATR > threshold

Performance Metrics

I track everything. Here are the key metrics:

Primary KPIs

  • Daily P&L: Target 10%, track actual
  • Win Rate: % of profitable trades
  • Profit Factor: Gross profit / gross loss
  • Sharpe Ratio: Risk-adjusted returns
  • Max Drawdown: Largest peak-to-trough decline

Secondary Metrics

  • Average win vs average loss
  • Trade frequency by strategy
  • Best/worst performing assets
  • Fee impact on returns
  • Slippage analysis

Lessons Learned

What I Got Wrong

  • Over-optimization: Curve-fitting to past data
  • Ignoring fees: High-frequency = high costs
  • No risk limits: Letting losses run
  • Single strategy: Markets change, strategies break

What I Got Right

  • Paper trading first: Prove it works before real money
  • Multiple strategies: Adapt to market conditions
  • Strict risk management: Preserve capital above all
  • Full automation: Remove emotion from trading
