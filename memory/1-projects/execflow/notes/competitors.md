# Competitor Analysis: EzClaw (Corrected)

## Clarification

**ezclaw.io** and **ezclaw.co** are **different companies**, not the same product.

| | **ezclaw.io** | **ezclaw.co** |
|---|---|---|
| **Company** | EzClaw | EasyClaw (different) |
| **Model** | Dedicated computer per user | Shared/free tier |
| **Price** | $69-99/month | Free to start |
| **Positioning** | Premium AI employee | Accessible AI assistant |
| **Vibe** | Warm, professional, stucco | Dark, modern, casual |

## What This Means

Two separate competitors with similar names, attacking the same market from different angles.

### ezclaw.io (The Premium Play)
- **Target:** Businesses, professionals, real estate, sales
- **Price:** $69-99/month (high margin)
- **Value:** Dedicated resources, reliability, "employee" framing
- **Risk:** Price excludes casual users

### ezclaw.co (The Volume Play)
- **Target:** Everyone else — students, casual users, experimenters
- **Price:** Free entry, upsell later
- **Value:** Zero friction, try before buy
- **Risk:** Free tier abuse, low conversion

## Implications for ExecFlow

### 1. Market Validation
Two companies with similar names means **demand is real**. The space is hot enough for multiple entrants.

### 2. Naming Risk
"EzClaw" vs "EasyClaw" vs "ExecFlow" — we need distinct branding to avoid confusion.
